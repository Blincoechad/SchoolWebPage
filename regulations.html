Sanctuary Regulations

Welcome to Pott's Off-Leash Dog Sanctuary! For the safety and enjoyment of all our furry friends and their families, please review and follow these important regulations during your visit.

Neutering/Spaying

Neutering and spaying are important. It prevents unwanted litters and reduce the number of animals that end up in shelters or euthanized. These procedures also lower the risk of certain health problems, such as uterine infections and breast tumors in females, and testicular cancer and some prostate issues in males. In many cases, they can also reduce behaviors like roaming, marking, and aggression.

Behavior

Aggressive dogs are not allowed. Dogs must be removed at the first sign of over-aggressive behavior to other dogs or humans.

Collars & Vaccinations

All dogs must wear a collar in the park at all times. Choke collars or prong collars are not permitted.

Dogs must have a current license tag and be immunized against rabies, distemper, bordetella, and leptospirosis.

Cleanliness & Environment

Dog feces and waste must be picked up immediately and placed in containers provided. Disposal bags are available throughout the park.

Holes dug by your dog must immediately be filled and covered up to maintain the park's condition for all visitors.

Handler Responsibilities

Dog handlers must be present in sight and voice control of their dog(s) at all times.

No more than three (3) dogs per handler are permitted per visit to ensure proper supervision and safety.

Leash Rules, Food & Beverages

All dogs within the fenced area must be off-leash to enjoy the sanctuary as intended.

No food or alcoholic beverages are allowed in the park. This policy helps keep the environment clean and safe for all dogs.

Children & Supervision

Children under the age of 16 must be under the direct supervision of an adult at all times while in the sanctuary.

Please follow these rules to ensure the safety and enjoyment of all park visitors, both human and canine.
